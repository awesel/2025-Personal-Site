Training LLMs to Predict National Survey Results

Andrew Wesel and Gheed El Bizri

We asked GPT-4.1 to reason and predict the distribution of responses to a national survey. Then, we picked the best reasoning traces and fine-tuned Qwen-2.5-7B on those examples. The results show significant improvement - our fine-tuned model achieves an average JSD (Jensen-Shannon Divergence) of 0.1, compared to 0.2 for the base Qwen model. Importantly, this site only shows examples where both models formatted their answers properly, but our fine-tuned model only had proper formatting about 40% of the time. We are considering reinforcement learning as a way to teach formatting (and better reasoning). Please email any questions to awesel [at] stanford.edu! Super happy to talk about this project.
